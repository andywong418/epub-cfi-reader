Bibliography

Hoare, P.J. (1995) Nuclear Magnetic Resonance, Oxford University Press, Oxford.
An introduction to the theory of NMR - a companion volume to this text.
Harris, R. K. (1986) Nuclear Magnetic Resonance Spectroscopy, Longman, London.
Akitt, J. W. (1992) Nuclear Magnetic Resonance and Chemistry, Chapman & Hall, London.
Two good introductions to modern NMR spectroscopy.
Fukushima, E. and Roederer, S.B.W. (1981)
  Experimental Pulse NMR - a nuts and bolts approach, Addison-Wesley,
Advanced Book Program, Reading (Mass.)
A user friendly introduction to pulse NMR - and a guide to building your own
spectrometer!
Abragam, A. (1978) The principles of nuclear magnetism, Clarendon Press, Oxford.
The NMR theorists “bible” - not for the faint hearted!
Harris, R. K. and Mann, B. E. (1978)
  NMR and the Periodic Table, Academic Press, London.
Mason, J. ed (1987) Multinuclear NMR, Plenum Press, London.
Laszlo, P. ed (1983) NMR of newly accessible nuclei, Academic Press, New York.
Three invaluable reference texts for the inorganic chemist.
Brevard, C. and Granger, P. (1981)
  Handbook of High Resolution Multinuclear NMR, Wiley Interscience, Chichester.
A valuable compilation of NMR data.
Derome, A. E. (1987) Modern NMR techniques for chemistry research, Pergamon, Oxford.
Freeman, R. (1997) Spin Choreography, Spektrum Academic Publishers, Oxford.
Two excellent reference texts dealing with all aspects of NMR spectroscopy with good sections on multiple pulse and 2-D methods.
Mann, B. E. (1986) Fluxionality of polyene andpolyenyl metal complexes, Chem. Soc. Rev., 1986, 15, 167, Royal Society of Chemistry, London.
Fyfe, C. A. Solid State NMR for Chemists, C.F.C. Press, Guelph, Ontario, Canada.
An excellent introduction to NMR in the solid state.
