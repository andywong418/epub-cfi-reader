Notice that, unlike in the previous example, the triple integral does not reduce to the product of three one-dimensional integrals. This is because the radius, or the maximum value of the r component, depends on the x–coordinate: r = f(x). Thus, while the angular -integral is self-contained, the radial and lateral contributions are interlinked through the limits; indeed, the sum is very difficult to formulate if the order of the r and x integrals is reversed.
