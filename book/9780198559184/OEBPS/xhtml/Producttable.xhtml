Square brackets [ ] are used to indicate the representation spanned by the antisymmetrized product of a degenerate representation with itself resulting from the Pauli exclusion principle and the indistinguishability of the two electrons occupying the same orbital.

Examples

The electronic states for t2g2 in an octahedral transition metal complex the direct product is:
t2g x t2g = a1g + eg + t1g + t2g giving rise to the states: 1A1g + 1Eg + 3T1g + 1T2g
For t2g 1eg1 the direct product is: t2g x eg = t1g + t2g giving the states: 3T1g + 3T2g + 1T1g + 1T2g
For eg2 the direct product is: eg x eg = a1g + a2g + eg giving rise to the states: 1A1g + 3A2g + 1Eg
