Preface

You have probably seen Periodic Tables with individual elements highlighted in some way or other in order to show, for example, the world according to the boron chemist, the lanthanide chemist or even the carbon chemist. The above Periodic Table highlights, in bold case, certain of the d-block, or transition, elements and it is the intention of this text to introduce the reader to the chemical properties of biological materials incorporating these elements—bio-coordination chemistry—and to the role that small molecule models can play in advancing our knowledge of the structure and function of transition metals contained in metallobiosites. It draws on other texts in the series for the basics of coordination chemistry, electron transfer, structural chemistry, and spectroscopy and so the assumption is made that the reader is familiar with them.

Sheffield D. E. F.
