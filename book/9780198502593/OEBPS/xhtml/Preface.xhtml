Preface

Solvents other than water are widely used by chemists. Undergraduate chemistry courses usually devote some time to discussing chemistry in non-aqueous solvents. Comprehensive undergraduate texts are usually limited in their coverage of the topic, and it is necessary to consult both inorganic and organic source books to get a more complete view of non-aqueous solvents. This primer was written to draw together the chemistry of non-aqueous solvents at a level suitable for undergraduates. It is specially suitable to accompany a module on non-aqueous solvents as found in many UK university chemistry courses. I hope it will also provide a useful short account of the subject for all chemists who use non-aqueous solvents in their work.

I would like to thank John Shorter and Peter Nelson who read the manuscript and made very helpful suggestions for improvements. Finally, I would like to dedicate this book to my wife, Barbara, for her love, encouragement, and support.

Hull J. R. C.
