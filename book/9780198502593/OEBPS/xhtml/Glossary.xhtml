Glossary and abbreviations

εrDielectric constant, relative permittivity
ηViscosity
κSpecific conductance
μDipole moment of a molecule
nDRefractive index (at λ = 589.3 nm)
pKautoNegative logarithm of autoprotolysis constant Kauto, equilibrium constant of 2HS ⇌ H2S++ S–
ANAcceptor number (see Section 1.5)
DMFN,N-dimethylformamide
DMSODimethyl sulfoxide
DNDonor number (Gutmann) (see Section 1.5)
EMFElectromotive force
EPAElectron-pair acceptor
EPDElectron-pair donor
HBAHydrogen-bond acceptor
HBDHydrogen-bond donor
HMPAHexamethylphosphoramide (≡ HMPT)
HMPTHexamethylphosphoric triamide (≡ HMPA)
RMMRelative molecular mass
THFTetrahydrofuran
Amphiprotic solventSolvent HS which can be protonated to to give H2S+ and deprotonated to give S‒
Dipolar solventSolvent consisting of molecules having a dipole
Electrolytic solventSolvent in which ionic compounds dissolve well to give electrically conducting solutions
Ion pairCation and anion bound together by electrostatic force, with small involvement of the solvent
Non-electrolytic solventSolvent in which ionic compounds dissolve poorly, and ion association gives low conductances.
Non-polar solventSolvent which does not readily solvate dipolar organic molecules, or inorganic ions
Non-protogenic solventSolvent which does not readily produce protons
Polar solventSolvent which solvates dipolar organic compounds, and inorganic ions
Polarizable solventSolvent where electrons move easily under the influence of an electric field
PolarizationResults of electron movement in a molecule as a result of an external electric field
Protic solventSolvent readily giving protons (≡ protogenic)
Protogenic solventSolvent readily producing protons
Protophilic solventSolvent with atom(s) containing lone pairs which are readily protonated
Protophobic solventSolvent which is not readily protonated
