Answers to problems

1a D–A → cyclohexa-1,4-diene; –H2 by retro-Cy. 1b EO of a cyclobutene, EC of a hexatriene. 1c GT of 2H2 to give 2 cis double bonds, 1,5-H shift 6.39 makes one trans. 1d Retro-Ch loss of CO; retro-D–A.

2.1 2-Me raises energy of s-trans 6.40 closer to s-cis raising proportion at equilibrium, the 1-Me group cis to double bond 6.41 reduces proportion of s-cis because of steric compression with C-4.

2.2a 1,3-D. 2.2b allylcation+diene. 2.2c H shift, pentadienyl cation+alkene.

2.3 D–A of C≡N to 3,6 positions; retro-D–A with loss of N2; D–A of C≡C to 2,5 positions; retro-D–A with loss of PhC≡N.

2.4 D–A of p-benzoquinone to cyclohexa-1,3-diene & intramol. [2+2] Cy hv; 2 × D–A of butadiene to acetylenedicarboxylate & intramol. hv.

2.5 Antiaromaticity of cyclobutadiene is lost 2×, and orbitals spaced better for good overlap to develop (C-l and C-4 of a diene are further apart).

3.2 2-step ionic competes with D–A; former faster in polar solvent.

3.3 [4+4] like 2.81, retro-D–A.; D–A, retro-D–A; 1,3-D, retro-D–A of CO2, intramolecular 1,3-D.

3.4 Base removes benzylic H, retro-’allyl’ anion+alkene, all-s gives trans-cyclooctene; retro [2+2], s on both σ-bonds makes trans-double bond. This is an exercise in drawing believable transition structures like 6.42.

4.2 EC con and EO con; ketone is cation-like: EO dis and EC dis.

4.3a EO con and EC dis → trans. 4.3b. 4.3b EO con and EC dis → trans.

4.3c EO con, D–A endo TS → ‘para’ all-cis adduct.

4.4a EO dis (photo) and thermal Cy [4s+2s]. 4.4b like 2.146 → 2.148, loss of H2 by retro Cy. 4.4c 4.51 → 4.53, D–A, retro D–A, and EO con.

5.1a Mislow and retro-Mislow, the sulfoxide somersaults from right to left.

5.1b LDA and TBDMSC1 make silyl enol ethers, then 2 suprafacial Ireland–Claisen steps. 5.1c Claisen then 2 × Cope, chair TS → trans double bonds.

5.2a EO of cyclobutene, [1,5]-H shift → cis double bond. 5.2b Chair Cope → cis-1,2-di-trans-1'-propenylcyclobutane, boat Cope → Me groups cis. 5.2c [1,5]-Shift of C, s with retention → leaves Me group in front, then [1,5]-H-shift moves double bonds so that neither is exoyclic to 6 ring.

6.1 Intramolecular ene delivers the H; retro-ene delivers the D. O3 → acetate.

6.2 Claisen → aldehyde; 2 possible ene reactions: side-chain ene and ring ene.

6.3 Ene like 6.20 makes cyclopropane, then the opposite retro-ene makes disubst. alkene.

6.4 EC con of octatetraene unit; EC dis of resultant hexatriene (cf. 4.47 → 4.49); intramol. D–A, s on both diene and dienophile.

6.5 EC dis of hexatriene → norcaradiene, and D–A gives left product; ene of cycloheptatriene with acetylene, then EC dis of new hexatriene → new noracaradiene, then Cope opens the cyclopropane.

D–A = Diels–Alder; EC = electrocyclic closing; EO = electrocyclic opening; GT = group transfer; Ch = cheletropic; Cy = cycloaddition; 1,3-D = 1,3 dipolar cycloaddition; con = conrotatory; dis = disrotatory; s = suprafacial.
