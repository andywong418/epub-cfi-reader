Bibliography

General inorganic chemistry texts

1Shriver, D. F., Atkins, P. W., Langford, C. H., (1994). Inorganic Chemistry, (2nd edn), Oxford University Press. (Now in its 3rd edn).

2Huheey, J. E., Keiter, E. A., Keiter, R. L. (1993). Inorganic Chemistry, (4th edn), Harper and Row, New York.

3Cotton, F. A., Wilkinson, G., Gaus, P. (1995). Basic Inorganic Chemistry, (3rd edn), Wiley, New York.

4Cotton, F. A., Wilkinson, G., Murillo, C. A., Bochmann, M. (1999). Advanced Inorganic Chemistry, (6th edn), Wiley, New York.

5Jolly, W. L. (1991). Modern Inorganic Chemistry, McGraw–Hill, New York.

6Greenwood, N. N., Earnshaw, A. (1984). Chemistry of the Elements, Pergamon Press, Oxford. (Now in its 2nd edn).

Texts dealing with structural inorganic chemistry

1Wells, A. F. (1984). Structural Inorganic Chemistry, (5th edn), Oxford University Press.

2Gillespie, R. J., Hargittai, I. (1991). The VSEPR Model of Molecular Geometry, Allyn and Bacon, Boston.

3Müller, U. (1993). Inorganic Structural Chemistry, Wiley, New York.

4Alcock, N. W. (1990). Bonding and Structure, Ellis Horwood, Chichester.

Texts dealing specifically with main group chemistry

1Massey, A. G. (1990). Main Group Chemistry, Ellis Horwood, Chichester.

Texts dealing with the understanding of Inorganic Chemistry principles and molecular orbital theory

1Owen, S. M., Brooker, A. T. (1991). A Guide to Modern Inorganic Chemistry, Longman, Harlow.

2Barrett, J. (1991). Understanding Inorganic Chemistry, Ellis Horwood, Chichester.

3Dasent, W. E. (1965). Nonexistent Compounds, Edward Arnold, New York.

4Winter, M. J. (1993). Chemical Bonding, Oxford University Press.

5Albright, T. A., Burdett, J. K., Whangbo, M. H. (1985). Orbital Interactions in Chemistry, Wiley, New York.

6Gimarc, B. M. (1979). Molecular Structure and Bonding, Academic Press, New York.

Books containing useful data

1Emsley, J. (1989). The Elements, Oxford University Press.
