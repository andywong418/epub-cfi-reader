PART ONE

Thermodynamics

 

 

Part 1 of the text develops the concepts of thermodynamics, the science of the transformations of energy. Thermodynamics provides a powerful way to discuss equilibria and the direction of natural change in chemistry. Its concepts apply to both physical change, such as fusion and vaporization, and chemical change, including electrochemistry. We see that through the concepts of energy, enthalpy, entropy, Gibbs energy, and the chemical potential it is possible to obtain a unified view of these core features of chemistry and to treat equilibria quantitatively.

The chapters in Part 1 deal with the bulk properties of matter; those of Part 2 show how these properties stem from the behaviour of individual atoms.
