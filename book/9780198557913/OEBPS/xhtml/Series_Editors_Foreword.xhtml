Series Editor's Foreword

Synthesis is the central area of organic chemistry where the chemist’s art and imagination are involved as much as his knowledge. As a consequence synthesis is a crucial topic for all students of chemistry but one they often find difficult.

Oxford Chemistry Primers have been designed to provide concise introductions relevant to all students of chemistry and contain only the essential material that would be covered in an 8-10 lecture course. This present primer by Christine Willis and Martin Wills presents the concepts of synthesis in a very logical and student-friendly fashion. This primer will be of interest to apprentice and master chemist alike.

Dr Stephen G. Davies
The Dyson Perrins Laboratory, University of Oxford
