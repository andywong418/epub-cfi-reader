Series Editor's Foreword

Polar rearrangements have always fascinated organic chemists. They have provided rich and rewarding areas of study particularly in the fields of biosynthesis, mechanistic studies, and stereochemistry. Many reliable and useful synthetic methods have resulted from studies of polar rearrangements.

Oxford Chemistry Primers have been designed to provide concise introductions relevant to all students of chemistry and contain only the essential material that would normally be covered in an 8-10 lecture course. In this fifth primer of the series, Laurence Harwood has done a magnificent job in bringing together the wide range of mechanistic, stereochemical, and synthetic topics that make up polar rearrangements. This primer will be of interest to apprentice and master chemist alike.

Stephen G. Davies

The Dyson Perrins Laboratory,University of Oxford
